# FIFA World Cup — Overview

The FIFA World Cup is the premier international men's football tournament,
organized by FIFA and contested by national teams. It has been held every four
years since 1930, with the only exceptions in 1942 and 1946 (cancelled due to
World War II).

- The first World Cup was hosted by **Uruguay in 1930**, and Uruguay won it.
- Through 2022, the final tournament featured **32 teams**: a group stage
  followed by a single-elimination knockout stage.
- The **2022 World Cup in Qatar** was the first held in the Middle East, and
  the first played in November–December rather than the usual mid-year window.
- The **2026 World Cup** will be co-hosted by the **United States, Canada, and
  Mexico**, and will be the first to feature **48 teams**.
- The trophy awarded today is the **FIFA World Cup Trophy**, in use since 1974;
  the earlier trophy was the **Jules Rimet Trophy**.
